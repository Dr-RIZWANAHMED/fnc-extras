f = @(x) 1./(1+x.^2);
x = @(t) sinh(pi*sinh(t)/2);
chain = @(t) pi/2*cosh(t).*cosh(pi*sinh(t)/2);
integrand = @(t) f(x(t)).*chain(t);
fplot(integrand,[-4 4])
set(gca,'yscale','log')
xlabel('t'), ylabel('f(x(t))') % ignore this line
title('Doubly exponential integrand decay') % ignore this line
This graph suggests that we may integrate t from to 4 and capture all of the integrand values that are larger than machine epsilon.
